UNDERSTANDING THE CHEMISTRY

Maltodextrin

Maltodextrin is a carbohydrate used as a food additive to improve texture and extend shelf life. It is derived from starch and is commonly found in processed foods.

INS 621 - Monosodium Glutamate (MSG)

Monosodium Glutamate (MSG) is a flavor enhancer commonly found in processed foods. It is derived from glutamic acid, an amino acid naturally present in many foods. MSG can enhance the umami flavor of dishes but may cause sensitivity in some individuals.

E300 - Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)

Ascorbic Acid, also known as Vitamin C, is an antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage. It is commonly added to foods to prevent oxidation and maintain freshness. Ascorbic Acid is also essential for the growth and repair of tissues in the body.
